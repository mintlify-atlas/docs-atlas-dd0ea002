Compliance is a shared responsibility. Medplum provides the platform controls, certifications, and documentation to support your compliance obligations — but your organization is responsible for how you configure, operate, and use the system.

This section describes the compliance frameworks Medplum supports, the controls Medplum maintains, and the materials available to help you meet your own requirements.

<Note>
  Compliance materials are publicly available where possible. For documents subject to NDA or partner agreements, contact [hello@medplum.com](mailto:hello@medplum.com) to request access.
</Note>

## Shared responsibility model

Compliance obligations are divided between Medplum and your organization.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What Medplum is responsible for">
    - Infrastructure security (physical data center security, network controls, patch management for managed services)
    - Platform-level encryption at rest and in transit
    - SOC 2 Type II audit and certification maintenance
    - HIPAA compliance of the Medplum hosted platform
    - ONC certification maintenance
    - Audit logging infrastructure (server-level `AuditEvent` generation)
    - Availability and disaster recovery of the hosted platform
    - Business Associate Agreement execution with customers
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What your organization is responsible for">
    - Access control configuration (who can access which data in your project)
    - User authentication strength (MFA enforcement, password policies)
    - Application-level audit log review and retention policies
    - Data classification and handling in your application
    - HIPAA training and policies for your workforce
    - Breach notification procedures
    - Compliance with regulations specific to your use case (e.g., state privacy laws, CLIA if operating a lab)
    - Self-hosted infrastructure security (if you are not using Medplum hosted)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Data protection

### Encryption

| Layer | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| Data in transit | TLS 1.2+ for all API and application traffic |
| Data at rest (hosted) | AES-256 encryption on all storage (RDS, S3) |
| Binary file access | CloudFront presigned URLs with time-limited access |
| Self-hosted S3 | Optional SSE-C with customer-provided AES-256 key |

### Audit logging

Medplum generates FHIR `AuditEvent` resources for authentication events and RESTful operations. These can be:

- Saved to the Medplum database (`saveAuditEvents: true`)
- Written to your log infrastructure (`logAuditEvents: true`)
- Streamed to a dedicated CloudWatch Log Group for long-term retention

See [Server Configuration](/self-hosting/server-config) for the relevant settings.

## Business Associate Agreement

Medplum will execute a BAA with any covered entity or business associate using the Medplum hosted platform. To request a BAA, contact [hello@medplum.com](mailto:hello@medplum.com).

<Note>
  The BAA applies to the Medplum hosted environment. If you are self-hosting Medplum, you are operating the infrastructure and are responsible for your own HIPAA compliance controls. Medplum can still provide guidance and materials to support your compliance program.
</Note>
